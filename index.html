The Art of the
Perfect Steep

Tea is not brewed — it is coaxed. Each variety asks for a slightly different temperature, a different patience, a different silence.

Warm the Vessel

Pour hot water into your cup or pot first, then discard. Cold porcelain steals heat before the first note can bloom.

Choose Your Water

Soft, filtered water at the right temperature — never boiling for delicate leaves. 70–85°C for greens; 90–95°C for blacks.

Measure Generously

One teaspoon per cup, but trust your nose. The dry leaf tells you everything about what it will become.

Honour the Time

Set a timer. Walk away. Return when ready. Oversteeping is grief; understeeping is impatience.
